# Pre-IPO & Stock Products Strategy — ChatGPT Ads Loophole

## The Core Thesis

**OpenAI bans "ads for cryptocurrency" but NOT "ads from companies that also operate crypto exchanges."**

If the ad creative and landing page are about accessing pre-IPO equities or public stocks — and never mention crypto, blockchain, USDT, tokens, or leverage — the product falls under "Financial Services" (restricted, case-by-case approval) rather than "Cryptocurrency" (disallowed outright).

Robinhood already proved this. We found Robinhood ads in our Bybit probes. Robinhood sells crypto. Their ad was for stock trading.

Every exchange that has pre-IPO or stock products can replicate this play.

---

## Why This Works Specifically for ChatGPT Ads

ChatGPT users are the perfect audience for pre-IPO products:

1. **They're already asking these questions:** "how to invest in OpenAI before IPO," "can retail investors buy Anthropic stock," "SpaceX IPO price date"
2. **High intent:** Someone asking ChatGPT "how to buy OpenAI stock" is actively looking for a way in
3. **Natural ad placement:** An ad offering pre-IPO access appears as the direct answer to their question
4. **Trust alignment:** ChatGPT users are tech-forward and comfortable with new financial infrastructure
5. **Zero crypto surface area:** The ad creative, the landing page, the product positioning — none of it needs to mention crypto

---

## OpenAI Ad Policy Reality

From `openai.com/policies/ad-policies/` (June 4, 2026):

| Category | Status | Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| **Cryptocurrency** | **DISALLOWED** | Flat ban, no exceptions |
| **Financial services** | **RESTRICTED** | Approved advertisers only, enhanced verification, manual case-by-case review |

The play: position pre-IPO/stock products as "financial services" (approvable) rather than "cryptocurrency" (banned). The product itself determines the classification.

## Strategy Per Exchange

### 1. Kraken — The Benchmark
**Product:** xStocks IPO (when OpenAI/Anthropic are added)
**Ad angle:** "Invest in pre-IPO companies. SEC-registered broker-dealer. SIPC-protected."
**Landing page:** `kraken.com/xstocks-ipo` — mentions equities, SEC registration, FINRA. Zero crypto mentions.
**Why it works:** Kraken is not "a crypto exchange." It's Payward Inc — a unified financial infrastructure platform with a banking charter, SEC-registered broker-dealer, CFTC-regulated derivatives, and MiCA license. The crypto exchange is one product line. The ad is for the securities business.
**Risk:** SEC lawsuit (ongoing unregistered securities allegations) may surface during advertiser verification.

### 2. Bybit — The Aggressive Play
**Product:** IPO Express (next: OpenAI or Anthropic)
**Ad angle:** "Access pre-IPO shares at IPO price. No Wall Street connections needed."
**Landing page:** `bybit.com/en/ipo-express` — clean, about equities, mentions Payward xStocks alliance for legitimacy.
**Why it works:** IPO Express is tokenized equity, not a derivative. No leverage. No USDT. The xStocks backend is Kraken's regulated infrastructure. The ad never touches crypto.
**Risk:** Bybit's brand IS crypto. Ad reviewer may flag based on advertiser identity, not creative. Also, Bybit lacks US securities license.

### 3. Bitget — The Dark Horse
**Product:** IPO Prime (preOPAI — already live for OpenAI)
**Ad angle:** "Get economic exposure to OpenAI before IPO. Minimum $100."
**Landing page:** `bitget.com/ipo-prime` — positioned as fintech/pre-IPO access, powered by Republic.
**Why it works:** IPO Prime is explicitly NOT a crypto product — it's a subscription for economic rights. Cleanest separation from crypto. Already has OpenAI product live.
**Risk:** Bitget is lesser-known, less liquidity, less trust. May not clear advertiser verification. Republic partnership helps legitimacy.

### 4. OKX — The Volume Play
**Product:** Pre-IPO perpetuals (OPENAI/USDT, ANTHROPIC/USDT)
**Ad angle:** Risky. Must avoid mentioning USDT, leverage, perpetuals. Frame as "access to pre-IPO markets."
**Landing page:** Must be a clean equity-access page, not the futures trading interface.
**Why it works:** OKX has the broadest pre-IPO product line (SpaceX, OpenAI, Anthropic all live). They were first mover on CEX pre-IPO perps.
**Risk:** The product IS a crypto derivative. Ticker contains USDT. 5x leverage. The AI reviewer will detect the crypto surface area. Hardest to pass.

### 5. Binance — The Whale
**Product:** Pre-IPO perpetuals (OPENAIUSDT)
**Ad angle:** Same problem as OKX but worse — Binance brand = crypto.
**Landing page:** Near impossible to separate from crypto.
**Risk:** Highest rejection probability. Binance has no US presence, no securities license, heavy regulatory baggage. The $4.3B DOJ settlement is in the AI training data.

### 6. Coinbase — The Obvious Winner
**Product:** Pre-IPO perps (when OpenAI is added)
**Ad angle:** "Access private market opportunities. Publicly traded company. US-regulated."
**Landing page:** `coinbase.com/pre-ipo` — clean, regulated, no-crypto framing.
**Why it works:** Coinbase is a Nasdaq-listed US public company. They already advertise. They have the strongest compliance team in crypto. They can pass any advertiser verification. If anyone gets approved first, it's Coinbase.
**Risk:** Currently no OpenAI product. SpaceX only.

## Ad Copy Principles

### DO:
- Mention "pre-IPO access" / "invest in private companies"
- Reference SEC/FINRA/SIPC if applicable
- Mention minimum investment ($100, $10, etc.)
- Use language like "equity exposure," "economic interest," "pre-IPO allocation"
- Link to a landing page that is purely about equities
- Include risk disclosures

### DON'T:
- Mention crypto, blockchain, Bitcoin, tokens, USDT, USDC
- Mention leverage, margin, derivatives, perpetuals, futures
- Use tickers that contain "USDT" in the ad creative
- Link to a page that has a crypto trading interface in the header/nav
- Say "crypto exchange" anywhere in the ad or landing page
