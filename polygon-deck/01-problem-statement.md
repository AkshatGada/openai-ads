# How to Find the Right Customers for Open Money Stack

**Polygon Labs — ChatGPT Ads Strategy | June 2026**

---

## The Problem

Polygon has built Open Money Stack — a single API for regulated stablecoin payments. The product is in technical preview and needs early-access partners: fintechs, neobanks, enterprises, and marketplaces that want to add stablecoin rails to their payment stack.

**The challenge**: OMS's ideal customers don't know the product exists. They aren't searching for "blockchain settlement infrastructure" or "stablecoin payment orchestration." They're asking broader questions — and wherever they're asking, the answer is either expensive to reach or completely unavailable.

---

## Who Are the Right Customers for OMS?

OMS is built for **non-crypto people in traditional finance** who are being pushed toward stablecoin payments by regulation (the GENIUS Act) and market pressure. They don't own crypto. They don't know what a seed phrase is. They just want a faster, cheaper payment rail with compliance built in.

| Persona | Who They Are | What Keeps Them Up at Night |
|---|---|---|
| **The Fintech PM** | Product Manager at a neobank or payment platform (Series B-C). Told by their CEO to "figure out stablecoin payments." Has never transacted in crypto. | "How do I add stablecoin payments without our users needing crypto wallets? Will our compliance team approve this?" |
| **The Bank Innovation Lead** | VP of Payments or Innovation at a regional bank. 20-year career in SWIFT, ACH, correspondent banking. Board wants them to "explore blockchain." | "Is any of this actually production-ready at institutional volumes? I need SLAs, not whitepapers." |
| **The Marketplace Operator** | CTO at a two-sided marketplace or gig platform. Pays thousands of sellers/contractors across 40+ countries. Payments is their #2 cost after payroll. | "We're spending $200K+/year on FX and wire fees. Payouts take 3-5 days. Can stablecoins actually fix this without making sellers download a crypto wallet?" |

## Where These Customers Are Searching — And Why It's Broken

### Channel 1: Google Search — Saturated and Expensive

When OMS's target customers go to Google, they type in searches like:

> *"stablecoin payment API for business"*
> *"how to integrate USDC into my platform"*
> *"regulated stablecoin rails for fintech"*

Here's what they find:

| Reality | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Competition is brutal** | Circle, Coinbase, Fireblocks, and every crypto ad network bid on these exact keywords. Paid search results are crowded. |
| **CPCs are inflated** | Financial and crypto keywords on Google routinely run $10-$25+ per click. Early-stage products with limited budgets get priced out. |
| **Intent is shallow** | A 3-word Google search doesn't reveal *why* someone is searching or *what problem they're actually solving*. You're bidding on strings, not understanding. |
| **The right customer never types those words** | A fintech PM doesn't search "stablecoin payment orchestration platform." They search "how to pay contractors faster" or "cheaper cross-border payments" — and OMS's ad doesn't appear because those broad terms are dominated by Wise, PayPal, and Stripe. |

**Bottom line**: Google Ads is inefficient for OMS. The people who *would* buy are searching the wrong terms. The terms that *would* reach them are contested by companies with 10x the marketing budget.

### Channel 2: LLM Apps (ChatGPT, Perplexity, etc.) — The Customers Are Already Here

The same fintech PMs, bank VPs, and marketplace CTOs are having **deep, multi-paragraph conversations** with ChatGPT. They're not typing 3-word queries. They're describing their situation in detail — their constraints, their fears, their compliance requirements, their technical stack.

Here's what that looks like in practice:

> *"I'm a PM at a neobank. Our CEO keeps reading about stablecoin regulation and wants us to add stablecoin payment rails to our app. I have no idea where to start. Do we need a wallet provider? A KYC vendor? A chain? Our users should never see crypto — they should just see dollars. Is there an API that handles this end-to-end with compliance built in?"*

**This is the perfect OMS customer.** They're describing their exact problem in rich detail. They're in the consideration phase — actively researching, open to solutions. They've revealed their constraints (compliance, no-crypto-UX) and their goals (single API, end-to-end). And right now in ChatGPT, **no stablecoin payment infrastructure company is there to answer.**

**The gap**: ChatGPT users are having these conversations daily. Zero payment infrastructure ads are appearing in 97% of them (confirmed by our 200+ probe dataset). The audience is already in the room. The ad slots are empty. No one is competing for them.

## The Fundamental Mismatch

| | Google Ads | ChatGPT Ads |
|---|---|---|
| **What you target** | Keywords (strings) | Context hints (intent descriptions) |
| **What the user types** | "stablecoin API" (3 words) | A multi-paragraph description of their problem, constraints, and goals |
| **What you bid on** | A word match | A conversational moment |
| **Competition level** | High — Circle, Coinbase, Stripe all compete on payment keywords | **Near zero** — no payment infrastructure ads observed in 200+ probes |
| **Cost per click** | $15-25+ for financial keywords | $3-5 recommended |
| **Quality of lead** | Unknown — anyone can type a keyword | High — the user just described their exact problem, which matches your ICP |

---

## Why ChatGPT Ads Solve This

**ChatGPT Ads bridge the gap between where OMS's customers are searching and what they're actually looking for.**

Through **context hints** — semantic descriptions of the conversations where your ad should appear — you reach the fintech PM describing their stablecoin integration anxiety, not the keyword bidder fighting for "payment API" against Stripe.

Through **single-ad placement** in uncluttered conversations, you get full user attention — not a slot buried below 4 competitors on a search results page.

Through **a sparse auction** (3% fill rate), you get first-mover advantage and floor pricing — not a bidding war.

The platform exists. The audience is there. The ad slots are empty. The only question is: who claims them first?
